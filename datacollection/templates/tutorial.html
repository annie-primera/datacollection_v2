How to use this grammar editor

Creating a new text

Click on the green button called "new text".

Writing your new text

You will be taken to a new screen with a very simple editor. You won't receive any feedback here, so you should focus on getting your ideas down.

When you have finished writing your text, click "save and check grammar" to begin receiving feedback. You will be able to edit your text later on, so this doesn't have to be a final version.

Summary and scores

The next screen will show you a summary of the feedback for your texts. Check the overall score you received, as you need a minimum score of 70 before you can submit your assignment. It also gives you some key actions that might help improve your text

This screen includes a lot of feedback, and some of it might be confusing at first, so focus mostly on the scores and the key actions. If you're curious about the rest of the feedback, follow the "Read more about this report" links for more in-depth explanations

Press the "Back to edit" button at the top of the screen to continue working on your text.

Dynamic feedback and editing the text

Here, you will receive detailed feedback in order to improve your text. Red lines under a word mean that there's a spelling mistake. Yellow lines are for grammar mistakes or style issues. If you hover over the underlined words, it will give you suggestions. The suggestions also come with an "ignore" button, which you are free to use if you don't agree with the program's suggestions.

You can use the "Summary" button to go back to the previous screen and see whether your score has improved.

If you want to save your text so you can work on it later, press the green "Save and exit" button, which will save your text and take you back to the dashboard.

If you're satisfied with the quality of your text, have achieved a minimum score of 70 and are ready to submit it to the instructor, press the blue "Submit text" button.

Your dashboard

All the texts you have saved will be accessible on your dashboard. You can click on them to review and edit them further, or you can delete texts that you're not satisfied with.
